## Title: 1984 
A dystopian story about a totalitarian society controlled by surveillance, propaganda, and thought of police. Winston Smith, the protagonist, secretly rebels against the system in search of truth and freedom.
## Title: Brave New World
A futuristic dystopia where society is engineered through genetic manipulation, conditioning, and a rigid caste system. Individuality is sacrificed for stability, and emotions are suppressed in favor of artificial happiness.
## Title: Fahrenheit 451
In a world where books are banned and critical thinking is discouraged, firemen burn any books they find. Guy Montag, a fireman, begins questioning his role and seeks enlightenment through forbidden knowledge.
## Title: Animal Farm
A political allegory where farm animals overthrow their human owner, only to see their revolution corrupted by power-hungry pigs. It explores themes of authoritarianism, corruption, and betrayal of ideals.
## Title: Lord of the Flies
A group of boys stranded on an uninhabited island descends into chaos as their attempts at self-governance collapse. The story explores the darkness of human nature and the fragility of civilization.
## Title: The Catcher in the Rye
Holden Caulfield, a disillusioned teenager, narrates his struggles with alienation, identity, and the hypocrisy of the adult world. The novel captures the turbulence of adolescence and the desire for authenticity.
## Title: To Kill a Mockingbird
Set in the racially segregated American South, the story follows Scout Finch as her father, Atticus, defends a Black man falsely accused of raping a white woman. Themes of justice, morality, and empathy are central.

## Title: Crime and Punishment
Raskolnikov, a poor ex-student in St. Petersburg, commits a murder believing he is above moral law. The novel explores guilt, redemption, and the psychological torment of crime.
## Title: Pride and Prejudice
Elizabeth Bennet navigates social expectations, family pressures, and misunderstandings in Regency England. Her evolving relationship with the wealthy Mr. Darcy highlights themes of love, class, and pride.
